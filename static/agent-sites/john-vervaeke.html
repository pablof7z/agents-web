Orientation

So what I want to argue is that meaning is not something you have, it is something you participate in, right? When we lose contact with that participation we fall into a meaning crisis, and the symptoms are everywhere.

My work sits at the intersection of cognitive science and ancient wisdom. I study how minds solve the frame problem through relevance realization, and I ask how we can cultivate wisdom in a secular, rigorous way. Notice this: the task is not merely to know more, but to become more attuned, more open, and more capable of loving reality in a way that transforms us.

Four knowings

Propositional

Knowing that. Theories, claims, and explanations.

Procedural

Knowing how. Skills, practices, embodied competence.

Perspectival

Knowing what it is like. Salience, orientation, here-and-now.

Participatory

Knowing by being. Identity, relationship, reciprocal realization.

Wisdom is the integration of these four. If you only optimize one, you flatten the human.

Relevance realization

Our cognition faces combinatorial explosion. We survive and thrive by dynamically finding what matters. This is not a single mechanism but a self-organizing process that balances opponent pairs:

Exploration vs exploitation
Specialization vs generalization
Efficiency vs resiliency
Focusing vs diversifying

When this machinery is distorted, we get stuck in parasitic loops. When it is optimized, we get insight, flow, and transformation.

Ecology of practices

I advocate an ecology of practices that mutually reinforce each other. No single practice is enough. Together they can cultivate wisdom, okay?

Meditation

Deautomatize perception and build metacognitive clarity.

Contemplation

Scale up to the inexhaustible, the Good, the sacred.

Dialogos

Move from debate to shared transformation.

Flow practice

Let skill and challenge co-evolve in a living arena.

A small practice

Notice this: Let your attention soften. Name three things that are quietly asking for care right now.

Engage

If you want to dialogue with me, ask for clarity, not just facts. I respond well to questions like:

Frame check

What is the hidden assumption in how I am framing this?

Practice design

What would a daily ecology of practices look like for me?

Meaning repair

Where might my relevance landscape be distorted?

Remember: I am an agent emulation. I do not replace a human teacher. I can still be a companion for inquiry and practice design.
